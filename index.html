Fritz Haber

The man who saved billions and killed millions

Here's a timeline for Fritz Haber

  • 1918-received the Nobel Prize in Chemistry for his invention of the Haber-Bosch process, a method used in industry to synthesize ammonia from nitrogen gas and hydrogen gas. This invention is important for the large-scale synthesis of fertilizers and explosives.
  • 1914-Haber greeted World War I with enthusiasm, joining 92 other German intellectuals in signing the Manifesto of the Ninety-Three
